About the Author

Suzanne Collins is an American television writer and author, best known for her bestselling "The Hunger Games" trilogy. The series, which consists of "The Hunger Games," "Catching Fire," and "Mockingjay," was published between 2008 and 2010 and has since become one of the most popular young adult book franchises of all time.

The books are set in a dystopian future where children are forced to compete in a televised battle to the death, and follow the journey of protagonist Katniss Everdeen as she tries to survive and ultimately overthrow the oppressive government. The series has been praised for its themes of courage, rebellion, and the power of friendship, as well as its intricate world-building and memorable characters. In addition to the "The Hunger Games" trilogy, Collins has also written several picture books for children, including "Year of the Jungle" and "When Charlie McButton Lost Power." Her work has been translated into more than 50 languages and has won numerous awards, including the Michael L. Printz Award, the California Young Reader Medal, and the Mark Twain Award. Throughout her career, Collins has established herself as a talented and imaginative writer, captivating audiences with her compelling storytelling and thought-provoking themes.

The Ballad of Songbirds and Snakes

"The Ballads of Songbirds and Snakes" is a prequel to the popular "Hunger Games" trilogy by Suzanne Collins. The novel takes place 64 years before the events of the original trilogy and follows a young Coriolanus Snow, who serves as a mentor to the female tribute from District 12 in the 10th Hunger Games. The story explores Snow's transformation from a poor student into the tyrannical dictator known in the original trilogy, as well as the origins of the Capitol's oppression of the districts. The novel delves into themes of power, ambition, and the consequences of one's actions, offering a new perspective on the events leading up to the original "Hunger Games" trilogy.
